JPMS

Journal Publication Management System — a system for running an academic journal from start to finish: authors submit papers, reviewers review them, editors make decisions, and accepted papers get published.

by Yongye Uy · CS394 group project

GitHub repo coming soon

what it doesOverview

Running a journal by hand (emails, spreadsheets, shared folders) gets messy fast. This project builds one place where everyone involved — authors, reviewers, editors, and admins — can do their part of the process: submit a paper, review it, decide on it, and eventually publish it.

Submit & review

Authors submit papers and upload files. Reviewers read them and leave feedback and scores.

Editorial decisions

Editors look at the reviews and decide whether a paper gets accepted, needs changes, or is rejected.

Publish

Accepted papers get grouped into issues and shown on a public page anyone can browse.

My role: Frontend. I worked on the pages for submitting papers, tracking reviews, and viewing manuscript files, matching a design our team had already planned out and gotten approved.

built withTech Stack

Laravel Livewire PostgreSQL MongoDB Docker

who can do whatRoles & Features

  • Author: submit a manuscript, upload new versions, invite co-authors.
  • Reviewer: get invited to review a manuscript, read the PDF, leave feedback and a score.
  • Editor: see all reviews for a manuscript and make the final decision.
  • Admin: manage journals, issues, and users across the whole system.
  • A PDF viewer built into the page, so anyone with access can read a manuscript without downloading it first.
  • Published articles show up automatically on a public listing page, grouped by journal issue.
